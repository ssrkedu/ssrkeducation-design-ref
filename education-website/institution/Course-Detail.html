Overview

The Diploma in Pharmacy (D.Pharm) programme prepares students for entry-level roles in pharmaceutical care, drug dispensing, and community health. Combining foundational sciences with practical training in pharmacology, pharmaceutics, and hospital pharmacy, the course emphasises hands-on learning through lab work, industrial visits, and clinical exposure.

Programme structure and course details ↓

Program Objectives

  • To impart quality pharmacy education aligned with PCI norms and industry needs.
  • To develop practical skills in compounding, dispensing, and safe medication use.
  • To foster ethical practice and patient-centred care in community and hospital settings.
  • To prepare students for licensure examinations and lateral entry to B.Pharm.

Eligibility Criteria

  • 10+2 or equivalent with Physics, Chemistry, and Biology or Mathematics from a recognised board.
  • Minimum aggregate as per state counselling and PCI norms (45% for General/OBC, 40% for SC/ST).
  • Minimum age: 17 years as on date of admission.
  • Valid rank in state-level pharmacy entrance / counselling where applicable.

Career Path

Graduates of the D.Pharm programme can pursue careers as:

Core Pharmacy Roles

  • Registered Pharmacist — Dispense medicines and counsel patients in retail or hospital pharmacies.
  • Hospital Pharmacy Technician — Support inpatient medication management and inventory.
  • Medical Representative — Promote ethical pharmaceutical products to healthcare professionals.

Further Study

  • Lateral entry to B.Pharm
  • Specialised certificate programmes in clinical pharmacy

FAQs

What is D.Pharm?

A two-year diploma programme recognised by the Pharmacy Council of India, focused on foundational pharmaceutical sciences and practical dispensing skills.

Can I open my own pharmacy after D.Pharm?

Graduates must register with the State Pharmacy Council and meet local licensing requirements before practising independently.
